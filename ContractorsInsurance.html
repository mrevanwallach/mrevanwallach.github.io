Contractors Insurance

If you're a contractor, and you want to take on a new project, you'll almost certainly be required by your client to provide a certificate of insurance. No insurance, no project.

They want to make sure that you're covered in case of an accident or a suit by a third party. If a client trips over a toolbox or one of your employees slanders a competitor, you could be on the hook for legal fees, damages, and more - whether or not you're found liable of any wrongdoing.

Generally speaking, this kind of insurance comes in handy when:

  • Property Damage: your employees damage a client's property, or a neighboring property, intentionally or inadvertently, on the job or off, your contracting company will probably be liable for expenses costs related to restoration
  • Third Party Injuries: a client trips over an extension cord and fractures their wrist, or slips on a wet floor in a work area, then that could end up being very costly for your company.
  • Reputational Injuries: a competitor comes after your company for one of a variety of reasons - whether it's because of an ad you ran or something you or an employee said off hand. Those legal battles can become very expensive very quickly.
